Ofsted/Statutory Inspection Of Anglican Schools (SIAS)

We were pleased to receive several letters congratulating the school on our recent SIAS inspection which is equally as important as the Ofsted inspection. Here are some extracts:-

  • Firstly from The Venerable Andy Piggott, the Archdeacon of Bath:

    “I would like to offer my warmest congratulations to you, your staff, governors and pupils, on the result of the SIAS inspection of Churchill School. To be judged as outstanding in all key areas of the inspection is a tribute to your leadership of the school, and the dedication of the whole school community.”

  • Secondly from Tess Gale, the Director of Education of the Diocese of Bath and Wells

    “The SIAS inspection focuses on the distinctiveness and inclusiveness of church schools and Churchill is certainly a school of which your local community can be proud. It gives me great pleasure to offer you our warmest congratulations on the outcome of your SIAS inspection last term. To be judged to be an outstanding school is a truly tremendous achievement, and is certainly testament to your leadership.”

This week Jane Neeche, HMI, visited our school. She would like to apologise on behalf of Ofsted for the delay in her monitoring visit which was due to take place six weeks after our full Ofsted inspection. The visit was postponed because of the snow and sickness of an inspector. I know the lateness of this visit has caused unrest amongst parents and that was shared with the HMI. We will receive her full report on the progress we have made since our inspection in about two weeks and hard copies will be sent to you. We would be delighted to invite you to a parents evening on Wednesday, 8th May to share HMI findings and the SIAS report. Governors will be joining us for this evening.
