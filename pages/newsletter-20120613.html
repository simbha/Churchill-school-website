Newsletter - 13th June 2012

School Diamond Jubilee Street Party

Wow! What a fantastic afternoon we all had. A special thank you to all who donated food and drinks, staff, Governors, PTA, who helped to make this such a memorable event. Another big thank you to Mrs. Strawbridge and Miss Roberts for making the costumes for the Maypole dancing and planning the party which flowed seamlessly throughout the afternoon.

Thank you to the Parish Council for the Jubilee mugs they presented to each child. The whole school photographs for each family were a gift from the PTA, we hope you will treasure them!

Worship

Next week the theme for our assemblies will be about looking after ourselves.

Village Jubilee

A huge thank you to so many PTA Committee and parents who cooked so skilfully throughout rain and shine – the onions were delicious. The event was very successful in bringing the village community together despite the soggy weather. Maisie, Edward and their attendants opened the fair with great professionalism. The school and Jubilee choir sang beautifully and the Maypole dancing by Year 1 was delightful. Thank you to Mrs. Strawbridge for making the outfits.

There were some very clever children and adult fancy dress outfits which added to the colourfulness of the day. The live music in the evening was outstanding so a special thank you to Garry Sharp’s band Deep River Blue.

Geraniums For Sale

If you would like to buy some geraniums to brighten up your garden they will be available in the playground on Thursday and Friday at 50p each, bags and honesty box provided! All proceeds to our village community.

Medicines In School

If your child needs to take any medicines during school time they should be given into the office and not stored in children’s bags. You MUST complete a “Medication Form” first (obtainable from the office) and one of our appointed first aiders will administer as necessary. Thank you.

Year 5 Folly Farm Residential

Year 5 children had a magnificent time at Folly Farm. Charles said:

“I enjoyed Folly Farm, Jo, our activity leader looked after us from 8.00 in the morning to 10.00 at night. She was great in every single way and so was Claire, unfortunately she was only with us for the first day. Folly Farm was the best 3 days of school I’ve ever had, from the hollowed out wood that looked like a pair of shorts, to the games of camouflage and the fire making well, that was just extreme. If I had the chance I would so go again!”

Fact Of The Week

There are more football clubs in England than anywhere else in the world. England also has the oldest football club, Sheffield. It has the oldest national governing body, oldest knockout competition, oldest national league and have produced the first national team. England’s Premier League is one of the most popular and richest sports leagues in the world and is home to some of the world’s most famous football clubs.
