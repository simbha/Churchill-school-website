As the days become colder and darker please remind your children of the need to bring a coat to school and to be vigilant when walking to and from school. We often have comments from members of the community about cyclists without helmets and children not walking on the pavements.

11.11.11

The children observed two minutes silence and learnt the history behind Remembrance Day. They also thought of current day members of the armed forces and their roles in future world peace. We sang the Peace Song and would like to share the chorus with you:

No wars will stop us singing;
Our voices will stay strong
Even through the darkest night
We will sing our song

No fear will stop us dreaming;
Our dreams will light the sky.
Even when all hope is gone
Our dreams will not die.

Well done to William Maitland-Round for playing the last post.

Talented Talkers

Last night two teams from Class 6 entered the Rotary Club Annual Youth Speaks competition. The team of Kerry Wilkinson, William Maitland-Round and Euan Taylor, shared their concern about the number of endangered animals there are now in the world and informed the audience of what they could do to help.

The Sportsmen team of Isobel Fowles, Chloe Blakemore and Euan Campbell, told the audience why they thought a little less money should be spent on the Olympic games in 2012 and a little more spent on sporting opportunities for young people.

Both teams were amazing and spoke confidently and enthusiastically about their chosen topics. Twelve teams took part in the competition so we are very proud to announce that The Sportsmen won overall. Well done to all!

Tom & Kylan’s Report

Tom Williams and Kylan Green reported on the visit of Mr. Salmon:

Tom

On Monday morning Class 4 were visited by Mr. Salmon, a retired vicar. Last week everybody wrote a question for Mr. Salmon to answer and we took it in turns to say our question. I asked him what it was like being a vicar and he replied, “Sometimes happy and sometimes upsetting”.

Kylan

Our favourite part was when James dressed up as a vicar. He wore a black cassock with a white surplice on top and around his neck he had a chasuble. James looked funny because it was way too big! Mr. Salmon told us he wore a dog collar so that everyone would recognise he was a vicar.
