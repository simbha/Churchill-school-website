National No Socks Day

Tuesday 8th May is National No Socks Day. We are going to use this national opportunity to raise funds for our partner school in South Africa. Children can come in their own choice of clothes wearing socks which are not on their feet, the brighter, more colourful, the better. How about wearing a sock on your hand. Pease bring a donation of 20p. Thank you.

Walk To School Week

The week 14th-18th May is Walk to School Week. Walk, scoot, cycle, hop, skip and jump - anything but motorised transport for the whole week! Daily tallies will be taken during registration apart from Wednesday when there will be staff at the two gates. A draw with prizes for those children who walk or otherwise all week will be held on Friday.

More About The Zoo Visit

What an amazing morning! The delight and enjoyment on the children’s faces was fantastic. We were so glad that the family who gave us this treat were able to join us for the event. The children learnt so much about animals’ habitats and the role of the zoo in our society now. Well done to everyone involved, especially the children who earned this treat through their improved lunchtime behaviour.

Emily in Year 5 said:

“We learnt about lots and lots of different animals, their habitats and diets. We learnt that a Madagascan hissing cockroach has no teeth and some stick insects eat the same food for the whole of their life.”

Year 1 commented:

“We all thought it was fun. Dave was great, he let us touch the cockroaches, they felt spikey. Beans the rat was really cute”.
