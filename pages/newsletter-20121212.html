Christingle Service At St. Mary’s

On Tuesday morning the children will be making their Christingles to share and use in our service at 10.45am at St. Mary’s Church in Langford. The Children’s Society introduced this family service in 1968. The orange represents the world, the red ribbon indicates the love and blood of Christ, the dried fruits and sweets are symbols of God’s creations and the lit candle represents Jesus the light of the world. Please come and join us and if you can help walk to the church please let your class teacher know. Thank you.

PTA News

The PTA would like to thank everyone for their amazing support on Saturday at the Christmas Fayre.

The day went fantastically well thanks to your donations for hampers, stalls and the tombola, not to mention delicious cakes and much, much more.

We would also like to thank the staff who gave up their rooms and their time on Saturday, the local residents, the stall holders, Pear Images Photographs, Mary Smallwood for the brilliant decorations and of course Santa for finding the time to fit us in.

We couldn’t have done it without all the parents who gave up their time to help set up, clear away and run a stall, so thank you, and thanks to everyone who enjoyed the afternoon and spent lots of money. The profits from the day look amazing and we will let you know the final figure in due course.
