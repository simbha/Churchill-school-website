Inspection Parents Evening

Last night we discussed the work we have undertaken since our Ofsted Inspection at our parents meeting. We were pleased to share the areas we have improved across the whole school and the impact we have had on children’s progress and achievement. We looked in more detail at the many positive comments in both the SIAS and Ofsted reports. Thank you to the staff, parents and governors who attended.

Some comments from the report that parents felt were important were:-

  • Achievement in reading is consistently good across the school.
  • The most effective teaching is enthusiastic, well planned and tailored precisely.
  • Pupils demonstrate consistently positive attitudes.
  • Staff manage behaviour skilfully.
  • The school is strongly committed to the nurture and care of all its pupils.
  • There is a real family atmosphere in the school and this due to everyone working closely together.

Governor Meeting Report

We had a very full agenda at our last meeting Kids Club Committee members Mrs Hill and Mrs McCoubra reported on the club activities for the year.

We reviewed pupil progress across the school and were pleased to see the vast majority of pupils are on a target to meet their July targets. Those who still have more progress to make are being given intensive support to improve their weaker areas.

We examined the progress of the Single Plan and checked all items had been completed.

We discussed the outcomes and actions from the SIAS report and celebrated the many very positive comments.

We learnt about the new requirements for assessment in Early Years and looked at pupils informative Learning Diaries.

All committees reported on their most recent activities and achievements and their impact on school life.

We introduced the budget for 2013/14 and arranged a separate meeting to enable governors to look in more detail at the proposed spend.

Thank you to the governors and staff for their attendance at the meeting.

Sally Furniss.
