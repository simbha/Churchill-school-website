Olympic Torch Competition

We are looking forward to seeing the torches you have made with your children. Please bring them into school on 18th May. All torches will join the parade, with one being chosen as the winner (by the school council) and leading us through the streets of Langford on 23rd May.

Jubilee Fair Sunday 3rd June

Now is the time to get ready for the red, white and blue fancy dress competition. Children in fancy dress can travel from the school in an old bus to the recreation field departing at 12.00.

National No Socks Day

What a wonderful array of socks were “worn” today, from Peru to homemade dragon faces. Thank you for the donations you made – a total of £30 will be sent to Woodford Primary School in South Africa so that the staff can buy socks for the children to wear in the coming winter months.

Walk To School Week

Don’t forget next week is Walk to School Week. Choose any way to come to school except motorised transport for the whole week! You can park in the Stag and Hounds pub and walk through to the back gate. Daily tallies will be taken during registration apart from Wednesday when there will be staff at the two gates. A prize draw for those children who walk, or otherwise, all week will be held on Friday.

National Teachers Day

We are very lucky to have such hard working teaching staff who go the extra mile for our children. Last week the children picked new names for our teachers using alliteration. The children had lots of fun. Why not ask them what they came up with?

Jubilee Fair - Events

The forthcoming Jubilee Fair promises to be a fun packed day. Some of the events taking place include Zumba, Morris dancing, children and ladies’ Maypole dancing, tug-o-war, worship and wheelbarrow racing. Cadbury Saxophone Band will also be playing throughout the day. If anyone would like to be involved in any way with the event contact Lesley Hegarty on 01934 852678.

Jubilee Quiz – Win £25!

The Village Fund committee are running a quiz as part of the celebrations in the run up to the Jubilee Fair. Quiz sheets are available at Churchill Post Office, the school office and the playground for £1 and can be returned to these places. First prize is £25 and the winner will be announced on Sunday, 3rd June at the Jubilee Fair.

PTA Disco

The PTA is running another very popular children’s disco on Thursday, 31st May. They have had plenty of offers to help with the KS1 disco but are struggling with the KS2 disco. If you are able to help please let a member of the PTA or the office know as soon as possible. If they don’t get enough offers of help the KS2 disco will not go ahead.

A Child’s View Of Lunchtimes

Oliver in Year 5 said that the school has lots of equipment to play on and we have really helpful lunchtime assistants. Matthew, also in Year 5, said that there was a lot of space to run around in and he likes the freedom to do so.

…Could Do Better

We will also be sharing ideas of how to take advantage of our local area as we endeavour to keep costs down. Watch out in September for the development of this area.

What We Do Well – A Parent’s Quote

This week the quote from one of our parents was that older children know their child’s name, mutual respect, ‘smallness’ of school, day trips and people brought into school, teachers' dedication and commitment to their child.
