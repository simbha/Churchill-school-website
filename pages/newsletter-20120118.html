Governing body

Your Governing Body works hard to support staff and pupils in making Churchill Primary School a great place to learn, work and play. This week we are continuing our series of governor profiles so that you may get to know your Governors better. You can also find out more on the school website – and look out for photos coming soon to the notice board.

Governors profile – John Votaw, Parent governor & Vice-Chair

“I became a Parent Governor in September 2006. I have a background in both teaching and management which I try to bring to the governing table. I feel parent involvement in the school is very important and was keen to add my voice.

“I work with the Facilities Committee to keep track of the current and future maintenance needs of the school. I also take a special interest in the school’s IT infrastructure and development. I served as Chair of the governing body for four years and was impressed with the skills and commitment of the other governors and found it very easy to work with such a motivated group. With a daughter in Year 6 and another in Year 1 I look forward to many more years of governing.”

Slap Cheek Syndrome

We are currently experiencing an outbreak of slap cheek in school. This illness is only contagious before the symptoms are visible. Advice from NHS Direct is that children do not need to be kept at home with this illness. Find out more by looking on the NHS Direct website.

The Giving Machine: not just for Christmas

You can generate free cash donations for the school every time you shop online via The Giving Machine. More than 100 retailers subscribe to this, including Tesco, Asda, Next, John Lewis, PC World, Toys R Us, Vodafone, eBay and Amazon and it will not cost you anything extra - the online shop pays a donation to the school on your behalf. Just go to www.TheGivingMachine.co.uk, join as a shopper/giver and choose to support Churchill Primary School, or call into the school office where they are offering the service for you.
