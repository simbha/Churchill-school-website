Newsletter - 21st June 2012

Jubilee Fair

A thank you from the committee —

We are delighted to announce that the Jubilee event raised about £2,000. This is in addition to the monies raised by individual stalls e.g. Rainbows and St Johns Church.

Some of the money raised will be put towards items that can be used by the whole village community in the future. Ideas already put forward include a marquee and new speakers suitable for outside use. Update to follow.

Please could you make sure that, when parking by the school, you avoid parking on grass verges, double yellow lines and are considerate to our neighbours.

Pink Day – Friday 22th June

A day to remember those who battle cancer. Children are invited to wear “something” pink instead of uniform. No donation is requested. This is just a reminder to encourage us to think of other people.

French Day

On Wednesday, 27th June we will be having a French Day in school. Children are invited to come to school dressed in bleu, blanc et rouge (blue, white and red). There will be a special French menu for those having a school dinner, traditional stories told by a French story teller for KS1 and KS2 and other surprises.

National Eat Your Vegetables

We hope your children are enjoying their vegetable tasting. Children are fascinated by plants growing so if you have a small pot of soil why not plant a bean or potato and let them see what you can grow in quite a short time.

We have vegetable seeds in school. If you would like some please get your child to ask at the office.
