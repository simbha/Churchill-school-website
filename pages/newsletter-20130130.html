Newsletter - 30th January 2013

Assembly

Next week’s theme is Lent. With Shrove Tuesday falling in our half term break, children will be making pancakes on 7th February. The Reading Rooms in Churchill will be serving pancakes on Tuesday 12th February from 2.30-4.30pm.

Certificates

Football: Joachim Hullah (Y2 ) and William Clamp (Y4). Well done for continuing to play in such poor weather conditions.

Puzzle Day

KS2 had an enjoyable day using educational games and puzzles to inspire their interest in maths. Children worked in small teams competing against each other to hone their maths skills. A super, amazing, challenging day was had by all. Thank you to the PTA for funding this event.

Year 5 Assembly

Year 5’s assembly followed the form of a newspaper report. Children picked out the highlights of their year. The children presented journalistic reports on their Business Enterprise challenge, their visit to the SS Great Britain as well as facts on Ancient Greece. Thank you to the parents for joining us for the assembly.

Year 5 T@3

Tea, coffee and delicious cakes were enjoyed by all those who braved the ‘icy weather’. Thank you to everyone who was able to make it, we raised £86.00 which will be put to good use.

Measure Your Feet Day

Year 4 had fun measuring their feet! They measured their feet in centimetres and millimetres and worked out how to record the measurement as a decimal. They then made a bar graph to compare the size of children’s feet in Year 4 with Year 3. The children then discussed their findings.

Popcorn Day

Children enjoyed eating their homemade popcorn last week. Thank you to the PTA for providing the ingredients and Mrs. Churchill for ‘popping’ it.

Just For Fun

On Tuesday we ate homemade chocolate cake. Although we are all trying to be healthy an occasional treat is always welcome. Watch out for a charming photo in this week’s Mercury.

Romeo & Juliet

The Old Vic Theatre Group put on a fantastic performance for KS2 children on Monday. Children and staff were spellbound although some of Year 6 thought there was too much kissing! A huge thank you to the PTA for funding this event. Years 5 and 6 from Wrington Primary School joined us.
