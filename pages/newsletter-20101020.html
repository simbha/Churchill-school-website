Newsletter - 20th October 2010

This term seems to have flown by with many exciting events leading to lots of learning.

Have A Look

On the Mercury website if you wish to see photos that have been taken of pupils in school. These are available for you to buy. The current reception class photo will be published after half term in the Mercury.

Apple Week

This week we have been looking at different varieties of apples grown in our country and around the world. Miss Schroeder will be cooking apples for the whole school! Did you know the Romans brought apples to England?

Very Well Done

Class 6 spent three days at Kilve Court experiencing outdoor activities and team building exercises. They came back tired but elated at their successful time.

Enjoyment And Learning

Staff spent two days working together to learn more about teaching children through a skills based curriculum. We were led by Tim and Maxine from North Somerset and the course was funded from the work that I completed at Sandford last year.

Folly Farm Report

Last Wednesday Year 3 went to Folly Farm. Oliver Wynn said "The best bit was making felt pictures because you get to hit it". Thank you to all the parents who supported T@3 as this helped towards the cost of the trip.

Useful Information

Governors met last week. They welcomed Karen Campbell to the governing body and she has joined the personnel sub committee. They discussed the School Improvement Plan (SIP) which has three key strands this year:-

  1. Creative Curriculum - Inspire children to be independent, innovative and reflective learners.
  2. Assessment - Evidence of Assessment Data used to influencing planning and practice, enabling all children to succeed and reach their full potential.
  3. Community and Church - to deepen links, relationships and communications with the Church and community.

They also took reports from committees:-

  • Curriculum had been looking at maths and marking in school.
  • Management had been working on balancing the budget.
  • Facilities had been working to complete the outside play area, planning and sun shelter.
  • Personnel have been looking at support new staff in post.

Thank you to the governing team for their work in our school.
