A Busy Week

Monday – Maths Multiplication Day

Multiplication Day was a great success challenging children to recite tables quickly, solve codes to work out tables and even a multiplication Sudoku (that one was left in the staff room!).

Tuesday – Christingle Day

We all made Christingles in our classrooms before visiting St. Mary’s Church to celebrate. The school councillors presented their Christingles to be lit and Harry Fear in Year 6 lit three of the advent candles. Boxes in the shape of candles were collected to raise money for the Children’s Society. We all enjoyed singing carols and celebrating Christingle. We would like to extend our warmest thanks to Mary Smallwood for donating all the ingredients for our Christingles and all the thoughtful preparation she provided. Louise Maitland-Round said:

“I really enjoyed watching all the Christingles being lit they looked so bright and Christmassy!”

Wednesday – Carol Service at St. John’s Church

Don’t forget you are all welcome to join us for the St. Johns’s Carol Service tonight at 6.00pm. Year 6 will be leading the service with contributions from Year 3 recorders, the choir and the staff chime bar ensemble.

Thursday – Christmas Lunch

Is our annual family Christmas lunch and as usual there will be two sittings; 11.45am for Reception, Years 1 and 6 plus siblings and family; 12.45pm Years 2, 3, 4, and 5 plus siblings and family. If you have booked to eat lunch, with your children please join your youngest child’s class for lunch collecting them from the playground before going to the hall. Due to the popularity of the lunch this year please do not come if you have not booked and paid.

Although we appreciate it can be tricky, please leave all pushchairs outside the school in order to comply with Health and Safety regulations. Thank you.

Friday – Carols around the tree

With only 6 sleeps until Christmas please join us for our carols around the tree this Friday at 2.30pm in the hall. Come and share our favourite festive carols along with tea/coffee and mince pie (for a donation) served in the library from 3.00pm-3.30pm, to finish for the festive season.

The Choir

Our KS2 Choir were treated to a delicious Christmas dinner at the Churchill Methodist Churchill Hall last Thursday. They were made to feel very welcome with crackers, turkey and all the trimmings. They sang some traditional carols beautifully after dessert. Lily and Mary said:

“It was lots of fun, we enjoyed the minibus and our dinner. We’d love to go back!”

Cinderella Rockafella

A fantastic performance from all the cast in Years 3, 4 and 5 and Year 5 were superb in their parts both speaking and singing. I am sure in a few years time we will have to look out for a few ‘stars in the making’.

Tia, who played ‘Buttons’ and Finley, who played Anastasia, said they really enjoyed the experience and had fun on the stage even though they were a bit nervous.

New LSA For Year 3

Mrs. McLean who has been working with us since September has moved on to a new post. We wish her every happiness. We interviewed on Friday and selected Mrs. Ford to work with us in KS2 from January 2013.

Inset Day Change

Due to our OfSTED report we will be moving our July inset training day to Friday, 8th February 2013. We apologise for any inconvenience but this date was the only one available to us.
