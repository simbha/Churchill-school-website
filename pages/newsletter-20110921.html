Newsletter - 21st September 2011

As autumn weather descends our thoughts are turning to harvest. We hope as many children as possible will join us for ‘home grown’ lunch on Thursday 29th September around mid-day. Get your slip in quickly as space is limited and booking closes tomorrow, and we need to know numbers for catering. Details and timings to follow.

Harvest

Our service this year will take place at St. Mary’s Church at 10:00 on Thursday, 29th for the whole school. Please join us (it will be a very full church) and take part in this delightful service of hymns, prayers, poems and readings. We are asking each child to bring a gift of food, home produce from your garden or dried goods; these will then be passed to the Children’s Hospice in Wraxall who will use them to make meals for the children and their families, so please give generously. Children should bring their donations to school on Thursday where they will then carry it to church.

International Peace Day

Today we marked this very special day which is working towards a day of cease fire and non violence world wide. The children sang beautifully…. We believe one day we will see a world in peace and harmony…. We are the future, we are tomorrow, we are the peace that you all crave.

Worry dolls

We have a dozen ‘Worry Dolls’ for children to borrow. Traditionally the children tell the dolls their worries and then put the bag under their pillow overnight. If your child would like to take one home they are in the main school foyer. Please ensure your child returns the doll after a couple of days. Thank you.

The Giving Machine

Nine people so far have signed up to the Giving Machine website. This is a site that lists major companies i.e. Amazon, Next, John Lewis, Sainsburys (to name but a few). When you enter these sites via The Giving Machine a donation will be made to your chosen beneficiary. It is simple and easy to use and a great way for the school to earn extra funds and it doesn’t cost you anything.
