Report from Sue Ivermee

Sue Ivermee (Senior Primary Advisor for North Somerset and OFSTED Inspector) wrote the following report after her visit on 23rd February:-

Lunchtime behaviour was observed. Pupils reported that ‘play fighting’ had stopped. Each class is working towards a whole school reward linked to lunchtime behaviour which will be a visit from Bristol Zoo. The lunchtime team were actively involving themselves in the outside activities. There now wear a very smart red jacket which means there are instantly visible. A recommendation made by the School Council.

Pupils have a good awareness of different forms of bullying and take active steps to prevent it from occurring. They know the school will act swiftly to address any incidents of bullying that do occur. Pupils feel safe at school. Pupils are considerate, respectful and courteous to staff and each other. At lunchtimes pupils assess and manage risk appropriately to keep themselves safe. There are marked improvements in behaviour reported by the pupils at lunchtimes. Pupils observed take turns, share resources and cooperate well with each other. Year 6 pupils have been given additional responsibilities. They are now acting as ‘role models’. This has provided opportunities for them to undertake additional responsibility at lunchtimes. Year 6 pupils act as role models for the younger children. They are very proud of their badges and recognise the clear expectations for this role and what happens if the forget to be a good ‘role model.’ Lunchtime Buddies are circulating more widely at lunchtimes if a need arises.
