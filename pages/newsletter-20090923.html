Newsletter - 23rd September 2009

This week we celebrated World Peace Day and the children thought of ways they could make their lives and the lives of others more peaceful. Thank you Mrs Williams and Ms Joskey for teaching the children the peace song:

No war will stop us singing ……
We are the future
We are tomorrow
We are the peace that you all crave …….

Children will be singing the full version of the song at KS2 Harvest Festival.

Quiz and Curry Night

Come along this Saturday and enjoy our PTA Quiz and Curry Night – tickets are £5.00 each. A bar will be available.

Playground Reminder

Please remember that before 8.45 a.m. and after 3.30 p.m. our school playground is not supervised. May we also remind parents that children should not play on the playground equipment or cycle/scooter around the playground for their own safety and the safety of others.

PTA A.G.M.

Members of the PTA Committee work very hard to raise funds and organise social events for adults and children throughout the year. Come and join us on October 13th at 8 p.m. and learn more about their work and maybe consider joining the Committee next year.

Healthy Snacks

Please encourage your children to bring healthy break time snacks. Fruit is available for KS1 children and many children in KS2 now bring fruit. We will be doing a “break survey” soon and will let you know the results.

Football

Well done to: Charles Lovell and Ben Wheeler, who were players of the week.

Cowboy Lunch

On Thursday October 15th there will be a change to the menu for school meals. The cowboy lunch will be: sausages, pasta, baked beans – mixed together and ice cream and cookies for dessert. Look out for the posters!

Harvest Lunch/Events

Come and join your child on Thursday 1st October at 12.15 p.m. for Harvest Lunch. Please fill in the slip below and return it to the school office in a named envelope together with £2.00 per person in payment to order a lunch. Children will be preparing soup and apple crumble on Wednesday 30th September and we would be delighted to have your help with the preparations. Please let Mrs Keenan in the office know if you can help. Money raised will go towards Children’s Hospice South West and supporting Woodford Primary School in South Africa. Donations of food i.e. cans, garden produce etc will be greatly appreciated on 1st October and will given to the Hospice after the event.

If your child normally has school dinners on that day and you have paid in advance, please still send in £2.00 for the Harvest Lunch and any credits will be credited to Term 2. Please note that this will be the only meal available on 1st October.

Harvest Services will be held on Thursday 1st October at St Mary’s Church Langford: KS1 at 9.45 a.m. and KS2 at 11.00 a.m. All are welcome to come along.

Macmillan Cancer Support

Macmillan Cancer Support are holding “The World’s Biggest Coffee Morning” at St Mary’s Church, Langford between 10 a.m. – 12.30 p.m. on Saturday 26th September, 2009. Everyone is invited to join in. There are lots of attractions including: Coffee and biscuits, Live Music, Book Sale, Helicopter flying challenge, Bakery, Balloon Race etc. Please support this very worthwhile cause if you can!

School Website

As we are working towards a paperless school office this year, your child’s “Topic Web” which gives details of what they will be learning this term, will be available on the school website. If you would like a printed copy, please ask your child’s class teacher. A huge “thank you” to all of the people who keep our website updated – we have had many positive comments about it.
