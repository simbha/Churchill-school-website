International Work

On Monday children spent the morning working in mixed age groups and visiting ‘6 countries’. In Ireland they ate soda bread and learnt an Irish dance. In New Zealand they performed their own Haka and sampled Kiwi ice cream. In Africa they had the opportunity to learn some drumming skills and sampled fresh pineapple. In Spain they learnt some simple greetings and tasted paella. In India they watched a traditional wedding and learnt about mendi (Indian hand painting) while eating chapatti, and when they were in Cuba children enjoyed a traditional tale and ate black beans. Thank you to all the staff for their help with preparing 1,200 food samples for the children. It was an amazing experience for our children.

Mrs. Lloyd

Mrs. Lloyd has been working at Churchill since 2000 and she has now decided, for personal reasons, to retire from full time teaching. We would like to thank her for all the work with children and staff. She has been a staff governor and Kid’s Club liaison representative as well as taking over the role of maths co-ordinator. She is always there at PTA events, children’s discos and the after school chess club has been popular. Her organisation and timetabling skills are legendary. She introduced many new elements to our curriculum including Folly Farm environmental studies. She will be missed by all of us and we wish her good luck for the future.

Aquathlon Achievement
