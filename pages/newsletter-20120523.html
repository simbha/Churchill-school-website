Village Jubilee — 3rd June

Children have been practising their singing and dancing, and we look forward to lots of families coming along to join the event. Worship at 11.00am with the opening ceremony at 12.00 noon. Our PTA are in charge of the BBQ so we can look forward to some succulent sausages/burgers with a pig roast in the early evening. Watch out for adult dancers who have been hard at work perfecting their steps.

Queen Maisie John, King Edward Cockram and their attendants – Angus Bird, Isaac Morris-Shapiro, Stan Hemmings, Libby Wilson, Danielle Mitchell and Lailah Knight.

They will be riding in an old bus from the school to the rec field departing at 11.50am. Any children in red, white and blue will also be welcome to join the bus – please note the bus is so old that it does not have seat belts.

So get your fancy red, white and blue clothes ready, join the Jubilee Spirit and spend the day enjoying the company of friends and family. Improvise on the inflatables, shop around the stalls, dance to the disco and Garry Sharp’s band, Deep River Blue. Children’s disco with DJ Ed at 5.30pm. Make this a day to remember.

If anyone would like to help for an hour on the day either setting up or helping to run a stall please let me know.

Fox Hunting — Y5

Emily Hurst has written:

“In class 5 we have been debating about fox hunting. Lots were for it but a good number were against. There were six groups which were local farmers, local residents, Fox Hound kennel owners, wildlife companions, local fox hunters and RSPCA representatives. We had four people come in and we told our arguments to them. They judged us and then they said that the local farmers were the best”.
