Lunchtime report

Report from Sue Ivermee, Senior Primary Advisor for North Somerset:

Pupils' behaviour is judged to be 'good' with outstanding features.

There is a clear plan in place to promote 'respect', with an expectation that pupils behave because they want to, not because they are told to.

Pupils are polite, welcoming and friendly. They relate well to one another. Pupils' attitudes are good; this is because of a range of successful strategies the school provides to engage learners.

Relationships are positive and the children report that they enjoy school and feel safe. The children said they like their lovely teachers and that they really enjoy school!

Pupils like the themed days and knew the next day was National Smile Day! A display in the entrance was stimulating and engaging asking the children and other members of the school community to capture what it is that makes them smile.

At lunchtimes the pupils go into the dinner hall from the playground sensibly and quietly. Older pupils take responsibility for the younger children. They play well together and engage purposefully with the resources provided. The playground is zoned and harmonious with areas for games, quiet areas and active areas with climbing equipment.

A positive approach to behaviour management is used throughout the school. The revised behaviour policy ensures routines are well established and expectations clear.

Pupils value the weekly cups that are awarded and want to win them. These include the Citizen Cup, the Cloakroom Cup and the Lunchtime Assistant Cup. The Citizen Cup is coveted; one pupil said he was "working towards it and hoped to win it soon".

We believe that lunchtimes can be even better and to this end we will be planning some changes to our lunchtime organisation; details at the start of next term.
