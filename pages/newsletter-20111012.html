Newsletter - Wednesday 12th October

Even though it was wet…

Thank you to all those who joined the walk to school this morning. A massive 66% walked. We do appreciate that it can be difficult to change morning routines, so please don't feel pressured into taking part - maybe next time.

Assembly

The children enjoyed wise words from four of our community leaders this week and the key skills they thought were important in leaders were organisation, attention, listening and encouragement. Next week's theme is feelings and friendships.

Lovely lunchtimes

This week we had the Principle Advisor from North Somerset and her colleague from the Vulnerable Learners team looking at lunchtimes in school. They were both impressed with what they "saw and heard". Once we have written details of areas they think we can improve, we will share our plans with you.

A smile is a curve that sets everything straight

On Friday we combined National Smile Day with National Poetry Day and asked all members of the school community “What makes you smile?”. In each class children combined their ideas to create different forms of poetry. Come and look at these poems on display in the next few weeks; we hope they make you smile too.

Governors profile - Ben Cuckson - Foundation Governor

"I joined the governing body in February 2010. With two children at Churchill Primary one of the reasons I became a governor was to play a more active role in what happens with their education and to support improvements to the children's education and their environment. I also have one child at Churchill Pre-School. I am a member of the facilities committee. Having studied Naval Architecture at Newcastle I now work for a ship safety organisation in the local area. In my spare time, I am a keen gardener, have recently taken up beekeeping and hope to get back into sailing, if time allows".

The Giving Machine - Shop online & raise funds for our school

You can generate free cash donations for the school every time you shop online via “The Giving Machine”.
