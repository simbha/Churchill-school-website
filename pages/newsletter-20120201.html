Governors profile - Lorna Vickery, Parent Governor & Vice Chair

I have three children – two of them are at the Primary School and the third is at Pre-School. I wanted to become a governor as I was interested in how children are taught in primary school, and how they are prepared for the big changes in their lives as they grow up, move to senior school and into work. I also wanted to contribute to the life of the school and felt this would be a good way for me to do so. I have been a governor since September 2009.

I am a member of the curriculum committee which allows me to develop my interest in how policies that are introduced by the government of the day are implemented and affect our children in school. As a governor my special subject area of responsibility is science.

I read Economics at Liverpool University before joining Tesco, initially in their property department where I was lucky enough to spend some time living in central Europe. When I returned I joined the marketing department, where I was marketing manager for the Clubcard scheme. I gave up work when I started a family.

Australia Day

We decorated our Round Table with all things Australian and learnt about the traditions and animals of the country. Many children also had a go on a didgeridoo, making some very interesting sounds and entertaining the office staff!

Chocolate Cake Day

Wow! What a treat we shared on Chocolate Cake Day! Each child tasted a chocolate sponge cake and a chocolate crispy cake. There were lots of interesting descriptions such as gooey, crunchy and yummy! Each class voted for their favourite with the sponge cake winning by a big margin in most classes. Our thanks go to Miss Schroeder who surpassed herself with the lovely cakes and always works so hard to provide the children with these experiences.
