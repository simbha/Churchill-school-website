Newsletter - 24th April 2013

Worship

This week we have been celebrating generosity and learning about St. George’s Day. We have a very generous parent body in school who constantly support us; our ‘garden group’, our PTA, our parent helpers and so many more, so a huge thank you from all of the staff. Next week our theme for worship will be Miracles.

Church Inspection

This week we celebrated our Outstanding Inspection with the children. Mrs. Holmes baked an amazing cake in the shape of a church which the children all ate with gusto.

OfSTED

We are now awaiting our monitoring report from Jane Neeche, HMI. In the meantime we continue our work to improve maths across the school including a twilight training session for staff which will be led by North Somerset on Thursday.

Certificates

Well done to Callum in Reception and Sam Ashford in Year 4 who were the footballers of the week. We will be giving out ‘double school certificates’ next Tuesday.

Spelling and Reading Workshop

Mrs. Grant, our literacy co-ordinator, will be leading a reading and spelling evening on Thursday, 25th April starting at 6.30pm in the school hall. Come and learn more about how we teach reading and spelling across the school and find out how you can support your child’s learning. Because of class teaching commitments we can only offer this event after school. We will follow up the meeting with an information sheet for all parents.

Tell A Story Day – Friday 26th April

We will be using our whole school worship time, 9.10-9.30am, to share stories with children. Four stories will be taking place in different parts of the school, so if you would like to join us please link with your child’s class. At break and lunchtime we will be telling more stories using our lovely amphitheatre and pergola areas. Stories are a hugely important way of increasing children’s vocabulary, so we ask you to promote books and reading at home.

Mini-Tennis

The mini-tennis planned for this Friday has been cancelled due to low interest from other cluster schools.

Year 2 Trip To St. Fagan’s

Last Wednesday Class 2 went to St. Fagan’s Open Air Museum in South Wales. We had great fun looking at different buildings throughout the ages and what they were used for. The highlight of the day was having a demonstration about shopping in the olden days by Mrs. Evans. Alfie said:

“I enjoyed the red house the most as it was the poshest!”

Mitu loved going over the Severn Bridge to get to Wales and Michael enjoyed the watermill. The museum is free to enter so if you have a spare day during the holidays Class 2 recommend it as a super day out for all the family! Just remember it is mainly outdoors, so a fine weather day would be best.

Year 3 Trip To Caerleon

Mary and Iris-May reported on Year 3’s trip to Caerleon and had an amazing time seeing Roman ruins. “We tried on some very heavy armour and a helmet. The Romans would have carried all this and more!” they reminisced. We just want to say a massive thank you to all the adults who came with us.

St. Georges Day

We celebrated St. George’s Day together, our round table has a stone dragon and we are encouraging children to design a dragon skin.

Norman

It is with sadness that we said ‘goodbye’ to Norman our Bearded Dragon this week. We held a short remembrance service and a small group of children created a lovely memory poster of Norman.

The dates are between 3rd and 12th May and will be open 10.00am-5.00pm daily. Ploughman’s lunches and homemade soup will be available from 12.30pm with drinks and cakes served all day.
