Newsletter - 14th November 2012

Remembrance Day

We were all so proud of the children for the quiet respect that they showed during this special time last Friday. Thank you to Kate Sax for leading the service at St. Mary’s Church. We hope that the wreath made and placed by the children will be a reminder for them over the coming weeks of the importance of working together as a community to ensure peaceful lives, Cheryl Curley kindly cut the wreath base for us. Thank you also to Mr. Young, our caretaker, who shared some of his family war medals for bravery with the children.

‘What A Hoot’ - Everybody Writes Day 2012

During the morning of Monday 22nd October, we were joined by Mr Jenkins and six of his feathery friends, including Spice the barn owl, Nyra the snowy owl and Bob the tawny owl! We learnt so much about their habitats, their diet and their amazing adaptations! Back in class we used our powerful persuasive skills to support Mr Jenkins in his appeal for a piece of land to set up a local owl sanctuary. We hope these letters and posters will help!
