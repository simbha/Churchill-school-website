OfSTED Update

This week we received our monitoring report from our HMI, Jane Neech. The visit looked at the progress and work we have made since the inspection. Her full report is available in the office or at our Inspection Update meeting on May 8th at 7.30pm. The most important paragraph is:-

You and your senior teachers and governors are taking effective action to tackle the areas requiring improvement identified at the recent section 5 inspection. Following the visit to the school, we agreed that further action was needed to:-

Ensure that the teacher with responsibility for leading mathematics, challenges teachers to guide pupils in lessons and mark their work so that pupils are clear about how to improve the quality of their work.

We were pleased to read that Jane Neech felt our action plan was “sharp with timely and appropriate actions”, that the school was being led in a determined manner and there a greater challenge especially for more able pupils.

Parent Questionaire Feedback Pupil Behaviour

Your comments ranged from:

  • Children are always kind and gentle.
  • Bad behaviour and bullying seem to be an on-going issue.

We have clear Behaviour and Bullying Polices, they are based on reconciliation techniques.

A small number of children have personal behaviour plans which include sanctions and rewards.

Behaviour is always judged at least good by agencies who visit numerous schools across the country.

North Somerset Principle Advisor (for all North Somerset Schools) July 2012

“The school has very high expectations for conduct and behaviour. Pupils’ behaviour and safety is outstanding. Pupils are helpful, considerate and consistently behave well.”

OfSTED October 2012

“Pupils demonstrate consistently positive attitudes to their learning and school life. Typically, pupils are polite and friendly, showing respect and kindness to each other regardless of age, race and culture. They report that bullying is rare but when there is some ‘silliness’ by a few pupils in the playground it is dealt with promptly and efficiently.”

SIAS March 2013

“Churchill is an outstanding Church school founded upon the Christian values of unselfishness, respect and responsibility. The school’s Christian values are aptly demonstrated in the attitudes and behaviour of pupils of all ages and abilities.”

Sometimes children may not know what actions have been taken by staff following a child’s “bad behaviour” and this can sometimes lead to the opinion that “nothing has been done”. We are working to ensure parents are kept better informed and told earlier if their child’s behaviour is not appropriate.
