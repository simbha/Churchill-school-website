Newsletter - 19th June 2013

Worship

This week our assemblies have been about the unknown and why we can’t understand everything that happens. Next week will be about changes incorporating the changing of classes for the new academic year.

Certificates

As these were not given out last week there will be double on Friday but well done to the footballers of the week Max (Y1) and Jack (Y3).

Busy Week For Year 6

This week Year 6 are taking part in some exciting activities in preparation for moving on to their relevant Year 7 schools.

Drama Day

Year 6 took part team building drama activities with children from other schools who be in their learning group next year.

Induction Day

Their first visit to the ‘big’ school where they had the opportunity to experience a day as a new student – science lessons were very popular.

Wells Cathedral

Thursday they will be spending the day at Wells Cathedral with pupils from other local Church schools.

Pink Day

Monday, 24th June is a celebration of anything pink so children can come to school dressed in pink or accessorise with ribbons, hair bands or socks. No school uniform required. Year 6 however will be having their school photograph taken so should wear school uniform.

Year R Class Assembly

Reception class had a wonderful assembly last Friday telling us all about the exciting work they had completed during the year. It was lovely to see so many parents enjoying their work. Thank you to the Year R team for making it such an enjoyable year for our children.

Golf

Last week Years 5 and 6 enjoyed a golf lesson led by a North Somerset trained coach. He inspired the children and taught them some interesting techniques.

French Day – Tuesday 18th June

Preparations began early to ensure French bread (thank you to the Cottage Loaf in Congresbury) and hot chocolate were ready for the children as they came into school. KS2 used their language skills to make bilingual picture books for KS1. KS1 enjoyed learning and singing French action songs. Thank you to Miss Schroeder for producing a ‘superb’ French lunch. We ended the day with KS2 sharing their books with the KS1 children.

Creative Writing

Congratulations to our Creative Writing team, led by Miss Barnes, who entered Radio 2’s 500 words story competition recently. A great experience for everyone.

We would also like to take this opportunity to congratulate Maddy Votaw (Y7), a previous Churchill Primary pupil, on making the shortlist for the top 50. A huge achievement if you consider the 96,000 entries! Well done Maddy.

Writing Competiton

Inspired by Radio 2 our Creative Writing club are running their own story writing competition. The stories will be judged in three age groups: R, Y1 and Y2; Y3 and Y4; Y5 and Y6. The only rule is that they must be 500 words or fewer! Ghost stories, mysteries, magical tales, adventures – anything goes! If your child would like to enter, stories should be posted in the competition box outside the office by FRIDAY 28TH JUNE at the latest!

Skipping Workshops

We have invited ‘Skipping Workshops’ to give a demonstration of skipping skills to the whole school on Friday, 28th June. The day will involve your child’s class in a skipping lesson focussing on the development of individual skills, partner work and long rope games. The Skipping Workshops Company has represented the UK in international skipping events and has appeared on TV on numerous occasions.

New Class Parents’ Evening

A parents’ evening has been arranged for parents to meet their children’s new teachers for the next academic year on Thursday 4th July – 6.00pm for Years 1 & 3, 6.30pm for Years 2 & 5 and 7.00pm for Years 4 & 6 and will last about 45 minutes. We hope you will be able to take this opportunity to see what exciting themes and topics will be available for your children next year.

The Giving Machine – Shop Online & Raise Funds For Our School

You can generate free cash donations for the school every time you shop online via The Giving Machine.
