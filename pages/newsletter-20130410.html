Newsletter - 10th April 2013

I hope everyone enjoyed the Easter break. We are all looking forward to an exciting Term 5, with plans for several special days in school during the term. It may be helpful to know:-

  • St. Georges Day – 23rd April We celebrate this day to remember that when faced with difficulties we should continue to be bold like St. George, patron saint of England. Come to school in Brownie/Scout uniform or wear something red, white or blue instead of school uniform.
  • No Sox day – 8th May We mark this day to remind ourselves of children in Africa who walk to school daily, through all weathers, all year, with no socks. Come in school uniform but without socks. If you wish to make a donation please give to your class teacher. All proceeds will be sent to Woodford Primary School, the school we are linked with in South Africa, to buy socks for their children.
  • For more information on forthcoming events see dates at end of this letter.

Worship

This week we are celebrating the wonders of Spring and considering the need to take care of our environment. Next week’s theme will be around friendship and treating each other with respect in our daily lives.

Easter Walk

Thank you to everyone who walked with us as we visited the five ‘stations’ of the Easter Story. The children were a credit to all of you; their manners, co-operation and behaviour were impeccable. Hattie and Connie in Year 4 said:

“We walked to the church to celebrate the last supper where we had bread and squash. We walked to the green and stood quietly and thought about the Garden of Gethsemane. We then went back to school and sang an Easter song and shared chocolate biscuits”.

Tag Rugby Tournament

Year 3 to 6 attended a tag rugby competition in Hutton Moor on the last Thursday of term. The children all played extremely well despite the weather conditions and won lots of their matches. Well done to all.

Football

Well done Tom Risk (Y6) and Oliver Walford (R) who received football certificates last term for their great team spirit and skills

Ofsted

We are still awaiting our HMI visit. In the meantime we have been working to improve the teaching of maths across the school and were pleased with the positive comments on our progress, after our North Somerset Maths Review Day. The review was led by Sue Ivermee, Head Primary Advisor, and two advisers from North Somerset Council. They spent the day in school observing the teaching, talking to children, looking at pupils books and the progress they had made. Thank you to the Governors who joined the team and took part in the interviews, also the staff for all their hard work in improving maths teaching across the school .

Questionnaire Feedback

We have grouped the comments on the questionnaires together as far as possible and each week will seek to address the points you have raised in the two categories ‘what you like’ and ‘what we could do better’.

What You Would Like Us To Do Better – Special Days

We celebrate special days to:

  • Raise children’s awareness of the wider world
  • Add fun to the curriculum and inspire children’s curiosity
  • Support charities
  • Increase children’s knowledge and understanding of the world
  • Enjoyment for all

Our special days are linked to Worship themes so we explain to children our reasons for celebrations. We will strive to make this clearer to all children.

Comments ranged from too many/too few/not enough notice/too expensive.

At the start of each school year we send out a list of dates for the year and we endeavour not to change these, occasionally we do add extras! The weekly school letter then gives fuller details of events coming up over the next month. We will now add more detail to the letters so guidelines are clearer.

What You Like – Positive Attitudes of Staff

Comments included approachable/friendly/fun and kind/KS1 induction really good/encouraging/fantastic teachers/easy to talk to about concerns/warm and friendly staff/open door policy/staff listen. Thank you.
