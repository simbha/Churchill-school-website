Little Reports

Year 2 Trip to St. Fagans

St. Fagans is a museum showing homes from the past. We went into a Celtic Village and a row of cottages from 1805 to 1985. Our best bit was when we went to a Victorian school where the teacher made you wash your hands and she checked them back and front and you had to sit up straight. They also only had two toys, one hoop with a stick, and the other was a ball on a string with a cup on the end and you swung the ball up and caught it in the cup. When it was cold the children carried a baked potato to school to keep their hands warm and when they reached school the teacher collected them in until lunch time.

By Charlotte Spensley and Isobel Vickery.

Playtex Moonwalk

On Saturday 15th May Mrs. Holdaway in Year 1 and about 17,000 other women will be taking part in The Playtex Moonwalk in London all wearing decorated bras! After weeks of training the extraordinary moonlit 26.2 mile marathon is through the heart of London raising money for Breast Cancer. Anybody wanting to sponsor her or make a donation are more than welcome. Do pop in and see her.

Picking Up

We seem to be getting a lot of calls at the end of the day when parents are unable to pick up their children. We would appreciate it if this could be done before the bell goes at 3.30pm as it is very difficult to find some children once they are out in the playground. We have had a few worrying moments where other parents have taken children home and we have not known! If you are taking a child who has been left in the playground please notify the office. Please remind your child that if they are not picked up they should go and wait in the library.
