Harvest

The school garden has been harvested; the vegetables have been prepared now all that remains for us to do is share our harvest feast! Our harvest service will take place at St. Mary’s Church at 9.45am. After wandering back to school and admiring the autumn colours we will settle down to a harvest meal of chicken and vegetable stew and fruit crumble. We look forward to sharing our harvest celebrations with you and your families.

Year 1 T@3

This Friday sees the turn of Year 1 to host the T@3. This is a once a term event that enables parents to get together at 3.00pm before they pick the children up and have a chat and cuppa. The host year will serve tea, coffee, homemade cakes and biscuits and all proceeds help towards something the class particularly wants.

The School Day

Now we are in full swing, we’d like to take this opportunity to remind you of our before and after school routines. In the morning the gates will be opened at 8.45am. When the weather is fine the children should remain on the playground where a member of the Senior Leadership Team (look for the ladies in red!) will be there to greet them. Children should not need to enter the school building to drop off bags or belongings until they are collected by their class teachers once the bell has gone at 8.55am. To encourage independence and responsibility we also ask that you let your child head into school and their classes by themselves. Scooters and bikes should also be left in the bike sheds before the children walk down to the playground using the path.

Children should always use the school gates and NOT the entrance through the reception UNLESS it is after 9.00am.

After school Key Stage 1 classes will be dismissed by their class teachers from classroom doors when they have spotted the friendly face of a parent or friend. Key Stage 2 classes are dismissed independently from their classes. Scooters and bikes can be collected but must not be used in the playground so as to prevent any bumps and knocks to toddlers. Thank you.

International Peace Day

“Peace lives in all of us. We just have to share it”.

Last Friday we joined millions of people from around the world to commemorate and strengthen the ideals of peace within and among all nations and people. In our service we shared peace hymns and spent a quiet moment reflecting on how we can create peace and harmony amongst our friends, family and in the wider community.

Open Afternoon

We ‘opened our doors’ a little wider this week when we opened up the school to the local community for an afternoon. Those who visited were shown around our warm and vibrant school by very proud Year 6 pupils. Visitors were also able to experience firsthand our ‘zest for life and learning’ as they joined children in their classrooms and wandered through our beautiful grounds and building. One visitor commented that “The children are very lucky to be in such a lovely school, I wish I was young again!”

Inconsiderate Parking

We have had a number of complaints about the parking outside school during the drop off and pick up times. Some of the cases reported include; double parking to let children out, letting children out on the road side and parking on the corners. In Rule 217 of The Highway Code it states that:

‘you should not park within 10 metres of a junction or on a bend’

The car park in the school grounds is for staff only and not for dropping off and picking up.
