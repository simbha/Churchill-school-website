Lunchtimes

As quoted from our recent Ofsted report ‘Pupils are polite and friendly showing respect and kindness to each other, behaving considerately around school.’

Lunchtime Assistants are linked to individual classes and give feedback to class teachers as to how playtimes have been, reporting on both the good and not so good elements. Very occasionally where the behaviour of individual children has been a concern a member of the Senior Leadership Team will investigate during the afternoon. These are recorded in a behaviour log (Black Book). Children are spoken to and their parents informed that evening to discuss further actions by a member of the Senior Leadership Team.

Holy Communion After School Club

Reverend Kate and some of the Open the Book team are offering an after school club for children aged around 7 to 11, to learn more about the meaning and practice of Holy Communion. Sessions will be interactive, fun and will help children learn more about this important aspect of church life and Christian faith. The school now hold termly Holy Communion service during Collective Worship. The aims of the club are to help children, their friends, staff and parents celebrate being part of the school alongside promoting our Christian ethos. The plan is for this course to run for 6 weeks and we would like to know which day of the week suits most people (not Thursday please!). Please let either Reverend Kate, Mrs Tighe or Mrs Hetherington know your choice of day for the Club. Thank you.
