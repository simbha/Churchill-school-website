Newsletter - 25th May 2011

Mrs. Isgrove

After many years of working at our school, Mrs Isgrove will be leaving at the end of this term. She has been an outstanding member of the staff team and will be greatly missed by children and adults. We wish her good luck in her future post.

Year 4 And The Amphitheatre

William, Matthew, Ellen-Mai and Charlotte from Year 4 would like to inform you:

"We helped Mr. Fletcher measure part of the field near our classroom. We are going to build an amphitheatre with steps that go up the bank. It will be used so that people can act out little plays at lunchtime. We'll tell you more later".

Assembly Theme

The theme for the first week back will be about sharing. This will encompass the school community, our village and extend to the world.

Fact Of The Week

Libby Wilson and Connie Rawlins in Year 2 found this interesting fact about teddy bears:

"Teddy bears are a source of comfort and warmth. They are even used by the police to help calm children who have been traumatised by accidents and crimes".

School Choir

We are looking for new members, old and young, to join our enthusiastic group who meet on a Monday afternoon. Please come to the office if you would like more information.

Our lost property bin outside Class 2 is again full of clothes, shoes and items we have found around school. If you think you may have mislaid something, please take a look. As it is coming to the end of the school year we will be taking any unclaimed items to the charity shop.

Churchill Memorial Hall

The Hall needs a new roof and the fundraising target is £25,000 - any donations would be gratefully received. Our school will be helping with fundraising in the new school year. If you are interested in keeping this valuable village asset then please attend a meeting to discuss its future on Friday, 17th June at 19:30 in the Memorial Hall. For more information contact Anne Williams on 01934 852430 or dferrierwilliams@tiscali.co.uk.

Children's Yoga Survey

Churchill Club 70 Fc

Churchill Football Club is raising funds in memory of former player Hugh McKerron (Jock) who died in June 2010. There will be a 5-a-Side tournament for past and present players on 28th May starting at 13:00 and all proceeds will be donated to Hearing Dogs for the Deaf. The Champions League final will also be screened live on the big screen at 19:45. For more information contact John Binding on 07802 760081.

From Wednesday Gang To Messy Gang

Messy Gang is replacing Wednesday Gang and their first meeting is Sunday 19th June. The Gang is aimed at Reception to Year 3 aged children for crafts, games, fun and food. Watch out for more information after half term.
