Newsletter - 5th October 2011

Harvest

Thank you to so many people who made our harvest celebrations outstanding from the arrangement in the church, beautiful flowers, crunchy veg, donations, enthusiasm, great lunch and so much more!! The Children’s Hospice came to our service on Friday and were truly grateful for the food donations. Mrs. Beadle is totting up the ‘pounds’ from the lunch and 50% will be sent to the hospice the other 50% will be sent to Woodford to buy seeds for their garden as they come into their Spring/Summer now.

Danielle and Jack in Year 4 wrote:

“As I walked into the playground carrying a bag of apples I could see lots of fruit and vegetables as well as tin cans and packets of food in people’s bags. Danielle was carrying a can of peas and one of peeled tomatoes. We were both excited to be going to the church. When we got to the church the table of food was full and looked very colourful. We started singing our favourite song The Harvest Samba and the service went really quickly. For Harvest lunch the soup was really yummy and the crumble scrumptious. I had lots of custard on mine. It was my first Harvest at Churchill and I really enjoyed it. Jack said it was one of his best ones too”.

Governors meeting

Last night was the first Governors meeting of the year. Sally Furness was elected Chair and John Votaw and Lorna Vickery as her Vice Chairs. We are currently looking for one parent governor – watch out for more information later this week.

Governors discussed the new School Improvement Plan (SIP) and learned more about how it will help your child achieve high standards and have happy lives at school. The three main strands are Creative Curriculum / Attainment & Assessment / Community & Church.

Governors are looking forward to a busy year full of hard work, laughter and progress.

Governors profile – Mrs. Jackie Bush (Curriculum Committee)

I have been a governor since 2001. As well as having five children of my own, I have been a foster carer for 25 years and am involved with the training of new carers. My first child came to Churchill Primary in 1993. I am a member of the curriculum committee and have special responsibility for special educational needs (SEN) and child protection. I am currently Chair of the Parish Council and served on the Council since 1997. I grew up in the village and attended local schools. My roles as a school governor and as a parish councillor demand energy and commitment, however they allow me to play an active role at the heart of the community.
