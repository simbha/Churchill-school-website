R.E.

Exciting things have been happening to our RE curriculum at school. Whilst all the children were enjoying the first day of their summer holidays, the teachers were joined by Dave Francis (who leads the subject for the whole of North Somerset) on their in-service day. He demonstrated the new website that has been created for teachers to use and explained how the new units are more investigational and start from posing interesting questions. Meanwhile Mrs Tighe has very kindly purchased some beautifully carved wooden crosses. They are all uniquely hand made and each class has their own to use for reflection and contemplation. Recently Key Stage 2 have been busy writing their own prayers to recite at the end of the day on a rota basis (so have Class 2!) and the rest of Key Stage 1 have been learning one altogether. A candle (battery operated so safe!) is lit just before home time and a prayer read out, a lovely way to end the school day! Here at Churchill we have also been very active in partaking in RE competitions, our old Year 6 took part in an art competition run by NATRE entitled Art in Heaven and although we did not win their entries were stunning! The current Year 6 is just about to enter a local competition where they have to design a PowerPoint presentation describing their ideal church. We wish them all the best!

Jeans For Genes Day

Walking into school this Monday you may have noticed something a little unusual. Many of us swapped our normal trousers and skirts for something a little more casual – jeans! Children and staff donned jeans in order to support ‘Jeans for Genes’ Day. This annual event, organised by Genetic Disorders UK, raises money to help support, care and change the lives of children with genetic disorders. With your support we raised £90, thank you!

In The Mornings…

As we continue to promote independent and initiative, we strongly encourage you to let your children head into school on their own once the morning bell has gone. Furthermore, if you’d like to talk to your child’s class teacher, please try to catch them before the 8.55am bell. After this time they will busy settling the children into their school day. Alternatively, you can always catch them at the end of the school day once your child has been dismissed.

School Health Nursing Team

The school nursing team now have a website that can be accessed by parents and children about the role and services that the team offer. These include National Child Measurement Programme, hearing and vision screening, things that pupils and parents might want to talk to us about, how to get in touch with the school nursing team for your school and links to the ‘No Worries’ teenage health service and other useful websites.
